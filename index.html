Real-time voting, multi-POV viewing, and audience influence

Interactive entertainment, in real time.

Creators can stream, publish prompts, set a location and time window, open real-time voting, and let audiences influence what happens while the experience is still unfolding. Flow

Viewers become part of the live experience.

Creators can launch a live prompt, define the location, set the participation radius, choose the time window for completion, and open audience interaction while the experience is still active. Prompts are normally evaluated after completion. Voting during an active prompt is limited to cases where the prompt is not being completed as intended, the result is disputed, or the situation appears unsafe or dangerous.

Examples

Live formats with real audience influence.

Ultimatum is not limited to one format. Creators can run city-scale tag, scavenger hunts, hide-and-seek pursuit, solo public prompts, audience-voted decisions, team-based live formats, and location-based business activations. Creators can set the exact location, the participation radius, and the time window users have to complete the prompt, which makes each experience feel tied to a real place and a real deadline.

Evaluation flow: prompts are normally evaluated after completion. Voting during an active prompt is limited to cases where the prompt is not being carried out as intended, the result is disputed, or the situation appears unsafe or dangerous.
Scavenger hunt

A creator sets a location, radius, and countdown, then sends participants to find checkpoints while viewers vote on which clue gets revealed next or which direction the search should go.

Solo public prompt

The audience votes between specific options such as cracking an egg on your head, eating a raw onion, or doing a timed reaction task, and the creator performs the selected prompt live.

Hide-and-seek pursuit

One player hides, another searches, and viewers influence the chase through route suggestions, pressure, or helpful guidance in real time, while switching POVs so they never miss the action.

City tag

Players move across a large area while viewers switch POVs, back a favorite participant, and influence who gets support or added pressure before time runs out.

Shops & real places

Location-based prompts can turn nearby attention into real visits.

Businesses can use location-based prompts to create a real reason for nearby users to show up. A café, restaurant, store, or venue can be set as the prompt location, while the creator defines the visible radius and the amount of time users have to complete it. That makes the experience immediate, local, and action-based instead of feeling like a static ad.

How this helps businesses: nearby visibility, creator-driven traffic, live urgency, repeat participation, and a clearer reason for people to walk in while the prompt is active.
Coffee shop

A café can launch a nearby-only prompt with a short timer: the first 10 people to arrive, chug a small cold brew on camera, and say the creator’s code phrase unlock the next live segment or appear in the stream.

Restaurant

A restaurant can host a timed food prompt, such as finishing a spicy wing round, doing a blind taste-test, or joining the creator’s live table segment before the timer ends.

Retail store

A boutique can run a live styling prompt where nearby users have a short window to come in, build an outfit, and let the audience vote on the best look.

Event venue

A venue can run a timed on-site prompt where nearby users must reach the location, find a creator checkpoint, and complete a fast task to unlock the next live segment.
